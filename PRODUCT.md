# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Northeastern University students — including incoming co-op, transfer, and exchange students — searching for short-term Boston-area sublets, usually under time pressure and often on a phone. Today they juggle Reddit, Facebook groups, Craigslist, and a handful of student housing sites, and they worry about scams and unverified posters. The job to be done: find a safe, relevant, affordable sublet near campus quickly, without checking ten different sites or getting burned by a bad actor.

## Product Purpose

1StopSublet aggregates short-term Boston sublets from multiple sources into one normalized feed, ranks Northeastern-relevant and near-campus listings first, and labels every listing by how much its source verifies the person posting it. It is an independent, unofficial tool — not affiliated with, endorsed by, or operated by Northeastern University. Success: a student finds a trustworthy, relevant sublet faster than scanning each source by hand, and can tell at a glance how much to trust each listing.

## Brand Personality

Trustworthy and calm. Voice: clear, plain-spoken, and safety-aware — never hypey, never salesy. Three words: trustworthy, calm, student-friendly. The interface should make a stressed student feel oriented and safe, earning credibility through clarity and honest source labeling rather than marketing flourish. The visual identity nods to Northeastern with a community-red palette while staying visibly independent (no NEU logos, seals, wordmarks, or "official" claims).

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS landing pages: gradient-on-white hero, the big-number hero-metric template, endless identical icon+heading+text card grids, tiny all-caps tracked eyebrows above every section.
- Cluttered, ad-heavy classifieds (old Craigslist): dense, sketchy, untrustworthy.
- Over-animated marketing sites that bury the actual listings beneath motion and copy.
- Anything that mimics Northeastern's official branding or implies an official affiliation.

## Design Principles

- **Trust is the product.** Every listing makes its provenance and verification level legible; never hide or launder where data came from.
- **Relevance first.** Northeastern and near-campus listings surface ahead of the noise; the feed respects a student's limited time.
- **Honest, not hypey.** No fabricated reviews, fake stats, or inflated claims. Show real aggregated data; when we can't do something yet, say so plainly.
- **Calm density.** Show enough to scan and compare quickly without feeling cluttered or anxious.
- **Independent by design.** Northeastern-community red as a nod, but unmistakably a third-party, unofficial tool.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast and large/bold text ≥3:1 — especially important with a saturated red palette over both light and dark surfaces; verify reds used for text, not just fills. Full light and dark themes already exist: respect `prefers-color-scheme`, persist the user's choice, and avoid a flash of the wrong theme. Honor `prefers-reduced-motion` for any motion added. Mobile-first: students browse on phones, so generous touch targets, full-width primary actions, and zero horizontal overflow are requirements, not polish.
